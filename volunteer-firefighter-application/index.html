Volunteer Firefighter Application

A service provided by FireDepartment.net to support volunteer fire departments.
If you are a volunteer recruiter and want to use this application for your fire dept, create an account on firedepartment.net (Instructions), update your contact info, add photos and add volunteer recruitment info.

Fill out the form below. FireDepartment.net staff will forward each submission to the volunteer recruiter's email address provided by the fire department. Many fire departments have additional requirements including a highschool diploma or GED, clean driving record, no felony convictions, etc. The fire department will request this information if they decide to move forward with the interview process.

Application Requirements:

  • Minimum age of 18 years
  • Valid Drivers License
  • Ability to perform physical firefighting and rescue tasks in adverse conditions
  • Ability to work cooperatively with other firefighters and supervisors
  • Ability to follow verbal and written instructions
  • Ability to keep calm and work effectively under stressful conditions
  • - -



  • Additional information may be required from the fire department to which you are applying. Please, do not submit your social security number, drivers license or other sensitive information on this form. Additional information can be submitted to the fire department you are applying, later in the application process.

    By submitting this form, you agree to share the information submitted above with the volunteer recruiter and/or fire dept profile manager on file for {departmentname} and FireDepartment.net.

    FireDepartment.net makes efforts to verify fire department profile managers. But, we don't verify the volunteer recruiter's info they post for the fire dept. The applicant accepts all risk associated with submitting an application.

  • Tips for aspiring volunteer firefighters:

    Don't assume being accepted as a volunteer is a given. Fire departments must commit a great deal of time and money into training each volunteer and outfitting them with gear. Plus, some fire departments in college towns, large cities or those with extensive training programs have many volunteer applications to choose from. So, it's important to create a good first impression and show you can follow directions.

    • Before submitting this form:
      • Take a couple extra minutes to review/revise your application.
      • Check capitalization and punctuation.
      • Ex: Capitalize the first letter of your first / last name.
    • Be sure that your voicemail is set up with a professional greeting
    • Consider putting together a resume and think about answers to questions you may be asked if the fire department gives you a call.
